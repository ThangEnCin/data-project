Conclusion, Tenant Data Manifesto & Data, Algorithms & Democracy

Data-driven housing platforms shape how users navigate the rental market and influence what forms of housing appear accessible. These systems can amplify inequalities through opaque sorting processes (Hintz et al. 2019; Srnicek 2017). To counter this, I propose a brief tenant data manifesto:

  • Right to Understand Algorithmic Decisions
  • Meaningful Data Sovereignty and Portability
  • Transparency around Data-Sharing Practices
  • Fair and Inclusive Access to Listings

Platforms like Rightmove act not only as search tools but also as intermediaries that shape digital participation and redefine what counts as legitimate user behaviour.
